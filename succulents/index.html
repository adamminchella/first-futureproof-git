Unlike cacti, which store water in their stems, succulents generally have large, fleshy leaves that retain water during periods of drought. When kept as houseplants, succulents should be planted in pots with drainage holes to avoid the risk of root rot.

Jade plant

Latin name

Crassula ovata

Water requirements

Water when the soil is dry. Reduce watering significantly over winter.

Light requirements

Will do best in bright indirect light but can tolerate full sun

Propagation

Stem and leaf cuttings, best chance of success in Spring/Summer

Soil

Plant will do best in a well draining, gritty soil

Repotting

Repot when rootbound, around every 2-3 years

Fertilization

Fertilize once a month in the growing season

String of pearls

Latin name

Senecio rowleyanus

Water requirements

Water when the soil is dry and the "eyes" on each pearl start to shrivel up

Light requirements

Full sun or bright indirect light

Propagation

Stem cuttings, best chance of success in Spring/Summer

Soil

Plant will do best in a gritty, well draining soil

Repotting

Repot when rootbound, around every 2-3 years

Fertilization

Fertilize once in spring
